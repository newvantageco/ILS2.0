## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a healthcare provider subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), and its implementing regulations, including the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E) and the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subparts A and C);

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides healthcare practice management software and data migration services to Covered Entity;

WHEREAS, in the course of providing such services, Business Associate will create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information ("PHI") on behalf of Covered Entity;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to comply with the requirements of HIPAA and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act");

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein contained, the parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** All capitalized terms used but not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the meanings set forth in 45 C.F.R. §§ 160.103 and 164.103, as amended.

**1.2** "Protected Health Information" or "PHI" means information that:
- (a) Relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual;
- (b) Relates to the provision of health care to an individual; or
- (c) Relates to the past, present, or future payment for the provision of health care to an individual;
- AND that identifies the individual or for which there is a reasonable basis to believe can be used to identify the individual.

**1.3** "Services" means the practice management software, data migration, and related services provided by Business Associate to Covered Entity as described in the Service Agreement.

---

## 2. PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES OF PHI

**2.1 General Use and Disclosure Provisions**

Business Associate may only use or disclose PHI:
- (a) To perform Services for or on behalf of Covered Entity as specified in the Service Agreement;
- (b) As required by law;
- (c) For the proper management and administration of Business Associate; or
- (d) To carry out the legal responsibilities of Business Associate.

**2.2 Specific Permitted Uses**

Business Associate may use and disclose PHI for the following purposes:

**2.2.1 Data Migration**
- To migrate patient data from legacy practice management systems to the ILS 2.0 platform;
- To validate, transform, and import historical patient records, examinations, prescriptions, and dispenses;
- To maintain audit trails of all data migration activities.

**2.2.2 Platform Services**
- To provide practice management software services, including patient scheduling, clinical records management, and billing;
- To store and process PHI in encrypted databases;
- To generate reports and analytics as requested by Covered Entity;
- To provide technical support and troubleshooting.

**2.2.3 System Operations**
- To perform data backups and disaster recovery;
- To monitor system performance and security;
- To conduct security audits and vulnerability assessments;
- To comply with regulatory reporting requirements.

**2.3 Prohibited Uses**

Business Associate shall NOT:
- (a) Use or disclose PHI for marketing purposes;
- (b) Sell PHI;
- (c) Use or disclose PHI for underwriting or similar purposes;
- (d) Use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate the Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**3.1 Non-Disclosure**

Business Associate shall not use or further disclose PHI other than as permitted by this Agreement or as Required By Law.

**3.2 Safeguards**

Business Associate shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as provided for by this Agreement, including:

**3.2.1 Technical Safeguards**
- Encryption of PHI in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256);
- Multi-factor authentication for system access;
- Automatic session timeouts and account lockouts;
- Intrusion detection and prevention systems;
- Regular security patch management.

**3.2.2 Administrative Safeguards**
- Security awareness and training programs;
- Access controls based on role-based permissions;
- Regular security risk assessments;
- Incident response procedures;
- Business Associate agreements with subcontractors.

**3.2.3 Physical Safeguards**
- Secure data center facilities with physical access controls;
- Environmental controls (temperature, humidity, fire suppression);
- Workstation security policies;
- Device and media controls.

**3.3 Subcontractors and Agents**

Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors or agents to whom it provides PHI agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate with respect to such information, including entering into a written agreement that complies with 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e).

Current subcontractors include:
- Cloud hosting providers (AWS, Railway)
- Database services (Neon PostgreSQL)
- Email service providers (Resend)
- Payment processors (Stripe) - limited PHI exposure

**3.4 Individual Access**

Business Associate shall make PHI maintained in a Designated Record Set available to Covered Entity (or as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual) within ten (10) business days of a request to enable Covered Entity to fulfill its obligations under 45 C.F.R. § 164.524.

**3.5 Amendment of PHI**

Business Associate shall make any amendment(s) to PHI in a Designated Record Set that Covered Entity directs within ten (10) business days of the request in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.526.

**3.6 Accounting of Disclosures**

Business Associate shall document and make available to Covered Entity within ten (10) business days information required to provide an accounting of disclosures in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.528, including:
- Date of disclosure;
- Name and address of recipient;
- Brief description of PHI disclosed;
- Purpose of disclosure.

**3.7 Breach Notification**

Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity of any Breach of Unsecured PHI without unreasonable delay and in no case later than ten (10) business days after discovery of the Breach.

Notification shall include:
- Identification of each individual whose PHI has been breached;
- Description of the breach incident;
- Types of PHI involved;
- Steps individuals should take to protect themselves;
- Mitigation steps taken by Business Associate;
- Contact information for individuals to learn more.

**3.8 Audit, Inspection, and Enforcement**

Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of Health and Human Services for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with the Privacy Rule.

## 4. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

**4.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures**

Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would not be permissible under the Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity.

**4.2 Notice of Privacy Practices**

Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate with a copy of Covered Entity's Notice of Privacy Practices and any changes or revisions thereto.

**4.3 Authorization and Consent**

Covered Entity shall obtain any necessary authorizations or consents from individuals before requesting Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in a manner requiring such authorization or consent.

**4.4 Restrictions**

Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any restriction on the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.522.

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

**5.1 Term**

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall terminate upon the earlier of:
- (a) Termination of the Service Agreement;
- (b) Mutual written agreement of the parties; or
- (c) As provided in Section 5.2.

**5.2 Termination for Breach**

If Covered Entity determines that Business Associate has violated a material term of this Agreement, Covered Entity may:
- (a) Provide Business Associate an opportunity to cure the breach within thirty (30) days; or
- (b) Immediately terminate this Agreement if cure is not possible or if Business Associate does not cure the breach within the cure period.

**5.3 Effect of Termination**

Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Business Associate shall:
- (a) Return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, that Business Associate maintains in any form;
- (b) Retain no copies of the PHI;
- (c) If return or destruction is not feasible, extend the protections of this Agreement to such PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible.

**5.4 Data Migration Upon Termination**

If Covered Entity terminates this Agreement and requests export of their data, Business Associate shall:
- (a) Provide a complete export of all PHI in standard formats (CSV, JSON, or FHIR) within thirty (30) days;
- (b) Verify data integrity and completeness;
- (c) Provide documentation of all exported data;
- (d) Destroy all remaining copies of PHI after confirming successful export.

**APPENDIX A: DATA MIGRATION SPECIFICATIONS**

**Covered by this BAA:**
- Patient demographic information
- Eye examination records and clinical findings
- Prescription history
- Dispense records
- Appointment history
- Clinical notes (text only)
- Billing and insurance information

**Excluded from this BAA:**
- Retinal images, OCT scans, or other diagnostic images
- Staff photographs
- Non-clinical documents

**Migration Process:**
1. Covered Entity requests data export from current vendor
2. Data received in encrypted format (CSV, Excel, or API)
3. Business Associate validates data completeness
4. Field mapping and transformation applied
5. Data imported into ILS 2.0 platform
6. Validation and verification by Covered Entity
7. Original data files securely destroyed after confirmation

**Security Measures:**
- Files encrypted during transit (TLS 1.3)
- Files encrypted at rest (AES-256)
- Access restricted to authorized migration team
- All actions logged in audit trail
- Original files deleted after successful import
